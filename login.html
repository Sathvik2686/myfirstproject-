How It Helps

When travelers in India book IRCTC train tickets, they often face situations where they must cancel their journey due to emergencies, sudden plan changes, or other reasons. Unfortunately, when they cancel these confirmed tickets, IRCTC typically deducts a cancellation fee, and the passenger only receives 50% to 70% of the ticket cost as a refund. This results in a financial loss for the traveler.

For Sellers (People with Confirmed Tickets Who Want to Cancel):

  • Instead of canceling and getting only a partial refund, they can resell their ticket to another traveler through IR-Swap.
  • This way, they can potentially recover more than the standard refund amount, maybe even the full ticket price.

For Buyers (Last-Minute Travelers):

  • Often, people plan travel at the last minute and struggle to find confirmed tickets.
  • IR-Swap connects these travelers with people who want to cancel, giving them access to already confirmed ticketsâ€”which might not be available through official channels anymore.

The Win-Win Situation:

  • Sellers get to recover most or all of their ticket cost.
  • Buyers get to travel with confirmed tickets even at the last moment.
